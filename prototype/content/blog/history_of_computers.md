NAME
    history-of-computers - an overview of how computers evolved with mathematics

SYNOPSIS
    history-of-computers

DESCRIPTION
    The history of computers is fundamentally intertwined with the development of mathematics. 
    From early counting tools to modern programmable machines, mathematical ideas have shaped 
    the architecture, logic, and theory behind computing devices.

    Early humans used tally marks and abaci to perform arithmetic, laying groundwork for 
    algorithmic thinking. In the 19th century, Charles Babbage conceptualized the 
    Analytical Engine — a mechanical general-purpose computer — based on mathematical 
    principles of calculus and algebra.

    The 1930s brought Alan Turing, who formalized the concept of computation with the 
    Turing machine, marrying logic and mathematics to define what it means to compute.

    Modern computer science continues to rely on discrete mathematics, logic, number theory, 
    and algebraic structures to develop algorithms, cryptography, and error-correcting codes.

OPTIONS
    There are no command-line options for this article, but understanding the mathematical 
    foundations is key to appreciating the power and limits of computing technology.
